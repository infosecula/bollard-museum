Frequently Asked Questions

"What can I expect to find at the Bollard Museum?"
Our museum stages public encounters between visitors, objects and stories. Whilst you may indeed find the bollards alone fascinating, our guided tour should help you discover much, much more. To put it more simply: There's a load of old bollards! (and some new ones)

"What are the museum's hours of operation?"
Our museum opens briefly, just once per week. To ensure your space please visit our Eventbrite page to book your tour

"Where is the Bollard Museum located?"
We are in the BS5 postcode of Bristol (Easton) close to Stapleton Road Railway Station and St. Mark's Rd

"Do you offer guided tours at the Bollard Museum?"
Whilst we are a thresholdless museum, we are open exclusively for guided tours, please visit our Eventbrite page to book your tour

"What types of souvenirs are available at the museum shop?"
We have a fun range of bollard-themed souvenirs, please visit our online gift shop

"Are there any special exhibits or events at the Bollard Museum?"
Each month we invite local artists to design and decorate some of our bollards, keep an eye out on our news page for information

"Is photography allowed at the museum?"
We actively encourage photography and filming, please share your images far-and-wide to help spread the word

"How can I support the Bollard Museum?"
Your presence alone is support enough for us, but if you would like to do more to help fund our work please consider purchasing a souvenir or buying us a coffee

"Are there any dining options near the museum?"
Lots! Nearby St. Mark's Rd and Stapleton Rd have an abundance of dining experiences covering many tastes and cuisines of the world.

"Are dogs allowed?"
You're welcome to bring along your well-behaved dogs, they must be kept on a lead and respect other people's space
